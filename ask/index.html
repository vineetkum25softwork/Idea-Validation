Product Idea Validation Sheet - The Explorer

Welcome to Your Product Idea Validation Journey! Please fill out the details below.

Section 1: The Core Idea

What's the name you're thinking of for your product or service? This is often the first thing people will know about your idea.

Example

Example: "Local Bakery App" or "Easy Home Cleaning Service"

What specific problem or challenge does your product or service aim to solve for people? Think about what makes people frustrated, inconvenienced, or what they wish was easier.

Example

Example: "Many people in my neighborhood want fresh baked goods but don't have time to visit the bakery during open hours."

Clearly describe what your product or service is. What does it do, how does it work, and what are its main features?

Example

Example: "My idea is a mobile app that lets users order fresh bread, cakes, and pastries from local bakeries and have them delivered directly to their homes. Users can browse menus, place orders, and track deliveries."

Section 2: The Target Customer

What is the typical age range of the people most likely to use your product or service?

Example

Example: "25-55 years old" or "Mostly teenagers"

What kind of jobs or roles do your ideal customers usually have? This helps understand their lifestyle and daily routines.

Example

Example: "Working professionals, stay-at-home parents" or "Small business owners, office workers"

What is the general income level of your target customers? This helps determine pricing and affordability.

Example

Example: "Middle-income families" or "Students with a limited budget"

What hobbies or interests do your target customers have that might relate to your product or the problem it solves?

Example

Example: "Cooking, healthy eating, supporting local businesses, convenience"

What are the habits, routines, or ways of thinking your target customers have that are relevant to your product?

Example

Example: "They often use delivery apps for food, prefer online ordering for convenience, or are too busy to run errands."

In which region are your customers available?

Example

Example: "Customers are available across North India, especially in bigger cities." or "Globally, for online digital products, but mostly in English-speaking countries."

In which region will you target your customers?

Example

Example: "Initially, I will target customers within the Patna district because of easier logistics and a strong local market." or "For the first six months, I will focus on the Delhi NCR region due to its large young population and high internet penetration."

Section 3: Market & Competition

What makes your product or service special or better than other options out there? Why should someone choose you?

Example

Example: "Our app delivers fresh bakery items within 30 minutes, directly supporting small, local bakeries by handling their deliveries for them."

Who are the other businesses that offer something very similar to what you're proposing?

Example

Example: "Other food delivery apps (e.g., Swiggy, Zomato), larger chain bakeries with their own delivery."

Who are the businesses or existing habits that solve the same problem in a different way, or are alternatives people might use instead of your solution?

Example

Example: "People visiting the bakery in person, baking at home, buying packaged bread from grocery stores, skipping baked goods entirely."

What are the strengths and weaknesses of your competitors?

Example

Example: "Direct competitors like Swiggy and Zomato have wide restaurant selection (strength) but charge high commissions to small bakeries and lack local focus (weaknesses). Indirect competitors like supermarkets offer cheap bread (strength) but lack freshness and variety (weakness). Our 'Local Bakery App' will differentiate by supporting local bakeries with better rates and offering specialized, quick delivery of fresh, diverse baked goods directly to customers."

Section 4: Business Model & Validation

How will your product or service make money? What will customers pay for, and how will that happen?

Example

Example: "We will take a small commission from each bakery order, and customers will pay a small delivery fee per order."

How will you find and talk to some of your potential customers to get their initial thoughts on your idea?

Example

Example: "I will talk to friends and family who fit the target customer profile, create a simple online survey to share on local community groups, or interview people near bakeries."

Once you reach out, how will you encourage people to try your product (even a rough version or concept) and give you honest feedback?

Example

Example: "Offer a discount on their first order if they provide feedback, give a small thank-you gift for completing the survey, or explain how their input will help shape the service."

What specific questions do you want answers to during your testing phase? How will you know if your idea test was successful?

Example

Example: "I want to know if people would pay for this convenience and how often. Success means at least 50% of surveyed people say they would use the app regularly."

Section 5: Promotion & Launch

How will you let your target customers know about your finished product or service when it's ready?

Example

Example: "Focus on social media marketing, local community outreach, and partnerships with bakeries."

What specific places or methods will you use to reach your target audience?

Example

Example: "Local Facebook groups, Instagram, flyers at local bakeries, community events, Google Search Ads for 'local bakery delivery'."

Why do you believe the channels you've chosen are the best way to find your target customers?

Example

Example: "Many busy professionals and parents are on Facebook and Instagram, making these good places to share about a convenient delivery service. Flyers at bakeries directly target people already interested in baked goods."

Section 6: Reflection & Next Steps

What are the biggest questions or challenges you still need to figure out?

Example

Example: "Ensuring consistent quality across multiple bakeries; scaling delivery logistics efficiently; acquiring initial users without a large marketing budget."
